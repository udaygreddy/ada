ADA — design & flow

ADP Discovery Agent · a portable skill a client runs in their own AI assistant to collect ADP onboarding documents, without ADP touching their systems.

One idea underpins the whole design: the client's model does the judgment; bundled stdlib code does the control. That split is what makes the audit trail trustworthy and the validation flexible at the same time.

The flow

From the ADP request to a client-transmitted package

Five stages. The amber stages are the point of the whole thing — they catch document problems locally, so the client fixes them before ADP ever sees the package. That is what removes the back-and-forth.

The architecture

Who does what — and why the trust holds

The model is flexible where documents are messy (reading a PDF, judging whether it matches). The code is rigid where correctness must be guaranteed (masking PII, hashing, gating). Neither can do the other's job — and that is the safety property.

The validation framework

Judged by the model, keyed by document type

Acceptance criteria live in validations.yaml, organized by doc_type and mined from ADP's onboarding guide. On a failure the operator immediately sees the exact, provider-specific re-export fix — no waiting for an ADP rejection email. New rules are added as data, not code.

Source of truth: PLAYBOOK-v2.md (host-neutral core), DESIGN.md (Paychex / Paylocity / Intuit instantiation), ada/validations.yaml (the acceptance-check catalog), and ada/PROCEDURE.md (the workflow the skill runs).
